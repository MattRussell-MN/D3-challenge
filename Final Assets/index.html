Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

When looking at the data, we do some strong correlations between health risks and age/income.

The first thing jumps out to me is income. When we compare this metric to both % of smokers and % of obesity, people in states who make less money tend to be more obese and smoke more. There are also some geographical patterns to this as well.

When looking at poverty, people in states that have a higher percentage of poverty, tend to have a higher rate of smoking and rate of obesity.
